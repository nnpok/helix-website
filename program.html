PROGRAM OVERVIEW


Through the Helix educational experience, students receive the training to become CPR certified, rotate through biological and clinical skills labs, and explore avenues for futures in health professions. Students will have the opportunity to interact with health professionals and be mentored by passionate, driven undergraduates. Because the program is student run, the staff is comprised of undergraduates with a heart for sparking and expanding a passion for medicine in youth of the Berkeley community, with the ultimate goal of encouraging marginalized high school students to pursue careers in healthcare.

INTERNSHIP


Following the week at UC Berkeley, students will be matched with four clinics of different specialties. This will give students the chance to explore first-hand what healthcare is all about, and experience different possible career paths. Accepted students will be expected to attend the one-week program and complete the entire four-week internship. Students may expect to spend four days of the week at the clinic, with hours subject to change based on each site’s hours of operation.



TRANSPORTATION

Students will be expected to find their own transportation to and from UC Berkeley and their internship sites.

TUITION

This program is offered completely free of charge. However, to keep the program running, donations of any amount are highly appreciated.

LOCATION

The one-week program will be held on the UC Berkeley campus. Each student’s internship sites will be revealed to them in their acceptance letter.

PARENT ORIENTATION


A parent orientation will be held at the end of May in order to inform parents about the program and get them involved in their child’s success. This will be held on a Friday evening from 6pm-8pm. At least one parent must be present for the student to be accepted into the program. This is absolutely mandatory and non-negotiable. We reserve the right to rescind your acceptance should you fail to attend this orientation.
